Denis Tchaouchev

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Political Science at Stanford University. I will begin a postdoctoral fellowship at the University of Pennsylvania Perry World House in August 2025.

I study the causes and consequences of international conflict in historical perspective. My current research explores the relationship between warfare and popular demands for redistributive taxation and the effect of technological change on the frequency of warfare.

In 2025, I worked as a Summer Associate at the RAND Corporation. Before graduate school, I developed fraud detection algorithms for Mastercard. I hold an MS in Computational and Mathematical Engineering from Stanford University and a BA in Computer Science and Political Science from Columbia University.

I can be reached by email at dtchaouc@stanford.edu
